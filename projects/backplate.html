Project Overview

This project investigates the structural behavior of a small 4-stroke internal combustion engine mounting backplate under representative operational loads. The objective is to evaluate stress concentrations, load paths, and mounting integrity using finite element methods.

Tools: Fusion 360, ANSYS Mechanical (2025 R2 Student)

System Context & Geometry

The backplate is part of a 30 cc model engine assembly. To establish realistic loading context, the component was first evaluated within the full engine geometry before being reduced to a standalone part for analysis.

Backplate shown within the complete engine assembly.


Reduced backplate geometry preserving bearing bores, fastener holes, and fillets.

Meshing Strategy

A tetrahedral solid mesh was generated with targeted refinement at stress-critical features, including bearing bores, fastener holes, counterbores, and outer fillets. Local sizing controls were applied to capture stress gradients while maintaining a reasonable global element count.

Refined mesh highlighting bearing bores and fastener locations.

Final mesh statistics:

  • ~75,000 nodes
  • ~40,000 elements

Loads & Boundary Conditions

The backplate was constrained at the mounting interfaces using remote displacement boundary conditions to represent attachment to the engine block. Bearing loads were applied using remote forces and moments aligned with a custom coordinate system representing the crankshaft axis.

Applied radial and axial bearing loads and mounting constraints.

Solver Limitations & Current Status

All geometry preparation, material assignment, meshing, and load application steps were completed successfully. However, solution execution was temporarily halted due to element type restrictions in the ANSYS Student license.

ANSYS solver message showing element type restrictions under the student license.

Despite this limitation, the pre-processing workflow demonstrates modeling decisions, boundary condition definition, and mesh refinement strategies.
