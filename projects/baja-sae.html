Baja SAE Roll Cage Structural Analysis

ANSYS Innovation Space — Guided Structural Analysis Course

This project documents the structural analysis of a Baja SAE roll cage performed using ANSYS Mechanical as part of an ANSYS Innovation Space engineering course. The chassis serves as the primary structural backbone of the vehicle, responsible for load transfer, stiffness, and driver safety under a variety of operating and impact conditions.

Tools: ANSYS Mechanical, SpaceClaim

Model Preparation

The provided roll cage geometry was imported and reviewed in SpaceClaim prior to analysis. The tubular frame represents a welded steel space frame commonly used in Baja SAE vehicles. Beam and pipe elements were assigned to the chassis members using appropriate outer diameter and wall thickness values to accurately represent structural behavior while maintaining computational efficiency.

Imported roll cage geometry used for structural analysis.

Load Cases & Analysis Overview

Multiple static structural load cases were evaluated to assess chassis stiffness, deformation, and stress distribution under representative competition and safety scenarios:

  • Front impact
  • Rear impact
  • Side impact
  • Rollover
  • Torsional loading

Each case was solved using linear static analysis. Total deformation and equivalent (von Mises) stress results were examined to identify load paths and critical regions within the roll cage.

Key Takeaways

This analysis highlights how different load cases activate distinct load paths within a tubular space frame. Impact scenarios concentrate stress near joint intersections, while torsional loading reveals overall chassis stiffness and resistance to twisting.

Although the geometry was provided as part of a guided ANSYS course, this project demonstrates hands-on experience with chassis modeling strategies, boundary condition definition, result interpretation, and engineering judgment applied to vehicle structural systems.

Next Steps

Future work includes applying these analysis methods to student-designed chassis geometries, such as Formula SAE or Shell Eco-marathon vehicles, where design iteration and optimization will be performed from first principles.
