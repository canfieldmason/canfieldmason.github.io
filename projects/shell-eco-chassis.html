Project Overview

This project documents the structural development of a prototype vehicle chassis for Shell Eco-Marathon, emphasizing lightweight design, structural efficiency, and data-driven validation.

Unlike highly standardized competitions, this effort involves original concept development informed by prior team experience and experimental validation.

Tools & Methods

CAD: SOLIDWORKS
Simulation: ANSYS Mechanical
Modeling: Beam, shell, and solid elements
Controls & Modeling: MATLAB / Simulink
Approach: Iterative design + experimental correlation

Current Status
  • Appointed Frame Lead for chassis development
  • Concept layouts and packaging studies completed
  • Awaiting senior team review of legacy designs and FEA models
  • Road load model in MATLAB/Simulink under development
  • Test track and dyno data integration planned

Engineering Objectives

  • Deliver a lightweight, structurally efficient frame
  • Validate design using FEA and experimental data
  • Establish reliable load cases from real-world testing
  • Improve stiffness-to-mass ratio over previous designs
  • Ensure compatibility with body, drivetrain, and suspension systems

Planned Analysis & Modeling Scope

  • Torsional and bending stiffness evaluation
  • Braking and cornering load cases
  • Powertrain and driver mass distribution
  • Joint and mounting interface stress analysis
  • Road load modeling using experimental data

All assumptions, boundary conditions, and mesh strategies will be documented to ensure repeatability.

Workflow

  1. Review senior team chassis and FEA models
  2. Develop validated load cases
  3. Create baseline CAD geometry in SOLIDWORKS
  4. Simplify geometry for analysis
  5. Run baseline FEA in ANSYS
  6. Correlate with MATLAB/Simulink road load model
  7. Iterate for mass and stiffness optimization
  8. Finalize manufacturable design

Next Steps

  • Consult senior team on legacy design choices
  • Review historical FEA and test data
  • Complete first-pass road load model
  • Begin initial CAD chassis layout
  • Establish structural benchmarks
