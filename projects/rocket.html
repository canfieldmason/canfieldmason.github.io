Madison Aerospace Club – Rocket Design & Simulation

Preliminary design and simulation work supporting a competitive student rocket program.

Project Overview

This project documents my contributions to the Madison Aerospace Club rocket team, with a focus on early-stage vehicle design and performance prediction using OpenRocket simulations.

Tools: OpenRocket

OpenRocket model used for preliminary performance and stability studies.

Completed Work

  • Explored the effects of fin geometry, body length, mass distribution, and motor selection on rocket stability and performance using OpenRocket.
  • Conducted trade studies to evaluate predicted apogee, stability margin, and descent behavior against competition requirements.
  • Contributed to preliminary configuration selection to support fabrication and flight testing in the upcoming semester.

Planned Next Steps

  • Finalize rocket configuration and support fabrication and assembly efforts.
  • Compare OpenRocket-predicted apogee and descent time with experimental flight data.
  • Quantify prediction error and refine simulation assumptions based on measured performance.

Current Status

The model and trade studies are complete and ready to support fabrication and flight validation. This project will be updated with flight data comparisons once testing is conducted.
