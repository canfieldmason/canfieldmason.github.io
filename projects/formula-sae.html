Formula SAE Chassis Structural Analysis

Completed through ANSYS Innovation Space

This project follows an ANSYS Innovation Space course focused on structural analysis of a Formula SAE spaceframe chassis. The objective was to develop correct structural modeling, load case definition, and result interpretation workflows commonly used in motorsport engineering.

The base chassis geometry was provided as a line network. The emphasis of the project was on proper geometry cleanup, tube property assignment, and evaluation under representative vehicle load cases rather than original chassis design.

Geometry Preparation

Line-based chassis geometry prepared in SpaceClaim, including cleanup of false intersections and refinement of node connectivity prior to structural modeling.

Tubular cross-sectional properties (outer and inner diameters) were assigned to each frame member to represent realistic chassis construction before meshing and analysis.

Torsional Stiffness Load Case

Torsional stiffness evaluation with equal and opposite vertical forces applied at the front lower wishbone pickup points.

This load case simulates chassis twist during asymmetric wheel loading. Total deformation was examined to assess torsional compliance and identify highly loaded structural members.

Cornering Load Case

Cornering load case with lateral acceleration applied at the center of mass and suspension pickup points constrained.

This analysis represents steady-state cornering conditions, allowing evaluation of global chassis stiffness and load transfer through the spaceframe.

Aero + Cornering Load Case

Combined aerodynamic and cornering load case with increased acceleration magnitude.

A higher acceleration factor was applied to represent combined aerodynamic downforce and cornering loads. Results were compared against the baseline cornering case to observe load scaling and structural sensitivity.

Frontal Impact Load Case

Frontal impact simulation with force applied at the front-most nodes and rear supports constrained. Resulting bending moment distribution during frontal loading.

This load case evaluates the structural response of the front chassis region under impact-like loading, highlighting load paths and critical members.

Engineering Takeaways

This project demonstrates applied use of ANSYS Mechanical for spaceframe chassis modeling, including line-body structural representation, realistic load case definition, and interpretation of deformation and internal force results within a Formula SAE context.

While the base geometry was provided, the workflow closely mirrors professional motorsport analysis practices and provides a foundation for future custom chassis design work.
